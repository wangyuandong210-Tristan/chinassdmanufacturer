How to Verify a Chinese SSD Factory: Quality Control Checklist

June 1, 2025 · 10 min read

When sourcing SSDs from China, the factory you choose determines your product quality, delivery reliability, and brand reputation. This checklist helps B2B buyers systematically evaluate potential SSD manufacturing partners.

1. Factory Ownership Verification

The first step is confirming the factory is a genuine manufacturer, not a trading company or middleman.

  • Business license - Request their business license and verify the registered address matches the factory location
  • Factory size - Ask for total floor area. A serious SSD factory should have at least 2,000-5,000 square meters of production space
  • Live video tour - Request a live video call showing the production floor, SMT lines, and testing equipment
  • Employee count - A legitimate SSD factory typically employs 50-200+ workers across production, QC, R&D, and admin
  • Years in business - Established factories with 5+ years of SSD manufacturing experience have proven track records

2. Quality Management Systems

A professional factory should have documented quality management processes in place:

  • ISO 9001:2015 certification - Verify the certificate is current and covers the products you're sourcing
  • IQC (Incoming Quality Control) - How do they test incoming NAND flash, controller chips, and PCB components?
  • IPQC (In-Process Quality Control) - What checks happen during SMT soldering, assembly, and firmware loading?
  • OQC (Outgoing Quality Control) - What final testing is done before shipping? 100% testing or batch sampling?
  • Traceability system - Can they trace each component batch to the finished product serial number?

3. Testing Equipment and Process

The testing infrastructure is a strong indicator of factory capability:

  • Burn-in testing - SSDs should undergo 24-72 hour burn-in at elevated temperatures to catch early failures
  • Performance testing - Speed tests (sequential and random read/write) should be done on every unit
  • Compatibility testing - Test on multiple motherboard chipsets and operating systems
  • Temperature cycling - For industrial SSDs, verify the factory performs -40C to +85C temperature cycle tests
  • Power loss testing - Test for data integrity during unexpected power loss

4. NAND Flash Sourcing

The source of NAND flash determines your product quality and customer trust:

  • Branded NAND only - Work with factories using genuine Samsung, Micron, Kioxia, SK Hynix, or YMTC NAND
  • Supply chain documentation - Request purchase orders or supply agreements showing NAND source
  • Grade consistency - Ensure the factory uses consistent NAND grade across all production batches
  • Counterfeit prevention - Ask about their process for verifying NAND authenticity

5. Product Certifications

Certifications are essential for legal compliance in different markets:

6. Communication and Support

A good factory partner is also a good communicator:

  • English communication - Do they have an English-speaking sales or project management team?
  • Response time - How quickly do they respond to email inquiries? 24 hours is standard
  • Sample process - Can they provide samples before mass production? What is the sample lead time?
  • Ongoing support - Do they offer post-sales technical support and warranty handling?
