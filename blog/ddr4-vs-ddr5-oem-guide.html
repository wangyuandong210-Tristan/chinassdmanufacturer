DDR4 vs DDR5 Memory OEM Guide: Which Should You Choose in 2026?

July 16, 2026 · 8 min read

If you are sourcing OEM memory modules for your brand, the DDR4 vs DDR5 decision directly impacts your product cost, performance positioning, and target market. This guide helps OEM buyers make the right choice based on current market conditions in 2026.

Key Differences at a Glance

SpecificationDDR4DDR5
Frequency Range2133-3600 MHz4800-7200 MHz
Voltage1.2V1.1V
Max Capacity per Module32GB128GB
CAS Latency15-1930-40
BandwidthUp to 28.8 GB/sUp to 57.6 GB/s
OEM Price (8GB module)$8-11$14-20

When to Choose DDR4 for Your OEM Line

DDR4 remains a strong choice for many products in 2026:

  • Budget-conscious markets - DDR4 is 30-40% cheaper than DDR5 at OEM pricing, making it ideal for entry-level and mid-range products.
  • Legacy platform compatibility - Many industrial motherboards and embedded systems still use DDR4 exclusively.
  • Large capacity, lower cost - A 32GB DDR4 module costs significantly less than a 32GB DDR5 module.
  • Stable supply chain - DDR4 production is mature, with consistent supply from multiple manufacturers.

When DDR5 Makes Sense

DDR5 adoption continues to grow, and it is becoming the standard for new designs:

  • Premium consumer products - High-end gaming PCs and workstations now expect DDR5 as standard.
  • Server and data center - DDR5's higher bandwidth and capacity are essential for modern server applications.
  • Future-proof product lines - New products launching in 2026-2027 should be DDR5-compatible.
  • Better power efficiency - DDR5 runs at lower voltage (1.1V vs 1.2V), reducing power consumption in laptops.

Market Adoption: DDR5 is Catching Up

As of mid-2026, DDR5 accounts for approximately 45% of new OEM memory orders, up from 25% in 2024. The price premium has shrunk from 80% in 2023 to roughly 30-40% in 2026, making DDR5 increasingly viable for mainstream products.

Recommendation for OEM Buyers

For most OEM buyers in 2026, the smartest approach is a dual-line strategy:

  • Offer DDR4 for budget and industrial product lines (lower cost, proven reliability)
  • Offer DDR5 for premium consumer and server lines (higher performance, future-ready)
  • Work with an OEM partner who can supply both, so you can adjust based on market demand
