How to Qualify an SSD OEM Manufacturer: A B2B Buyer's Guide

July 15, 2026 · 12 min read

The right question to ask yourself is not "Can this factory make SSDs?" but "Can this factory make SSDs that protect my brand's reputation?" This guide helps B2B buyers systematically qualify SSD OEM partners in China.

Why OEM Partner Qualification Matters

A poor SSD OEM partner can cost you in product returns, shipping delays, and brand damage. For example, one buyer we know sourced from an unverified factory and received SSDs with counterfeit NAND that failed within 3 months. He lost $40,000 in chargebacks alone.

Step 1: Verify the Factory's Credentials

Start with these three verifications before any serious conversation:

DocumentWhat to VerifyRed Flags
Business LicenseRegistered address matches factory locationAddress is a virtual office or shared space
ISO 9001 CertificateCurrent date, covers SSD manufacturingExpired certificate or generic scope
CE/FCC/ROHS ReportsIssued by accredited lab, your product modelGeneric or obviously copied certificates

Step 2: Audit the Production Floor

Even a video call can reveal a lot about factory capability:

  • SMT lines - How many lines? What brand of pick-and-place machines? A professional SSD factory should have at least 4-6 SMT lines with Samsung or ASM equipment.
  • Burn-in chambers - Every SSD should go through 24-72 hour burn-in. Ask to see the chambers and the testing log.
  • ESD protection - Workers should wear ESD wrist straps and anti-static shoes. This is a basic requirement for SSD assembly.
  • Warehouse and inventory - How is NAND flash stored? Temperature and humidity controlled? Branded NAND boxes visible?

Step 3: 10 Questions to Ask Your OEM Partner

Ask these specific questions during your qualification call:

  1. What NAND flash brands do you use? Can you show supply agreements?
  2. What is your production capacity per month for NVMe and SATA SSDs?
  3. Do you do 100% burn-in testing or batch sampling? What is the burn-in duration?
  4. What is your product defect rate? Can you provide last year's quality report?
  5. How do you handle OEM branding? Minimum order quantity for custom labels?
  6. Can you provide CE/FCC/ROHS with my brand name on the certificate?
  7. What is your typical lead time from order to shipment?
  8. Do you offer samples before mass production? Sample cost and lead time?
  9. What is your warranty policy for OEM products?
  10. Can international buyers visit your factory? Do you support video tours?

Step 4: Request and Test Samples

Before any production order, always test physical samples:

  • Test performance (CrystalDiskMark or similar) - does it match the claimed speeds?
  • Check the NAND marking - open the SSD and verify the NAND flash brand and marking
  • Run a burn-in test on the sample for 48 hours at 60C
  • Check compatibility with multiple motherboard chipsets
  • Verify the OEM labeling quality - is the printing clear and aligned?

Conclusion

Qualifying an SSD OEM partner takes time, but it prevents costly mistakes. The best partners will welcome your questions, provide documentation readily, and offer factory visits. If a factory is evasive about any of the items above, consider that a red flag.
