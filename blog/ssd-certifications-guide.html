SSD Certifications: CE, FCC, ROHS - What B2B Buyers Need to Know

April 18, 2025 · 12 min read

When importing SSDs from China, understanding certification requirements is critical. Products without proper certifications can be held at customs, fined, or even banned from sale. This guide explains the key certifications and what B2B buyers need from their OEM manufacturer.

CE Certification (European Union)

CE marking is mandatory for all electronic products sold in the European Economic Area (EU + EFTA). It indicates that the product meets EU health, safety, and environmental standards.

  • What it covers - Electromagnetic compatibility (EMC), low voltage directive (LVD), and RoHS compliance for SSD products
  • Who needs it - Anyone selling SSDs in EU markets (retail, online, or B2B)
  • What to request - A CE Declaration of Conformity (DoC) listing the specific SSD model, applicable directives, and standards
  • OEM consideration - Your factory should provide CE certification under your brand name for OEM products

FCC Certification (United States)

FCC (Federal Communications Commission) certification is required for all electronic products sold in the United States. SSDs fall under FCC Part 15 for unintentional radio frequency emissions.

  • FCC SDOC - For SSD products, the manufacturer can issue a Supplier's Declaration of Conformity (SDOC)
  • FCC ID - If the SSD contains wireless features (uncommon for standard SSDs), an FCC ID is required
  • Label requirement - The FCC marking must appear on the product or its packaging
  • Testing - Requires testing at an accredited EMC laboratory to verify emissions limits

ROHS (Global)

ROHS (Restriction of Hazardous Substances) compliance restricts the use of lead, mercury, cadmium, and other hazardous materials in electronic products.

  • ROHS compliance is required in the EU, but similar regulations exist in China (China ROHS), California (Prop 65), and other regions
  • SSD manufacturers should provide a ROHS test report from an accredited laboratory
  • OEM buyers should ensure their factory uses ROHS-compliant components and soldering materials
  • Some buyers also request REACH compliance documentation alongside ROHS

How OEM Factories Handle Certifications

Reputable Chinese SSD manufacturers typically include certification handling as part of their OEM service:

  • Certifications under your brand - The factory should provide CE, FCC, ROHS certificates with your brand name and product model
  • Certificate transfer - If the factory already has certifications for the base product, they can issue a certificate transfer or SDOC in your brand name
  • Physical labels - The certifications should appear on your product labels and packaging
  • Electronic copy - Request PDF copies of all certificates for your customs clearance and retailer compliance teams

Quality Standards Beyond Certifications

In addition to regulatory certifications, look for these quality indicators:

  • ISO 9001:2015 - Quality management system certification for the factory itself
  • Burn-in test reports - Documentation showing 24-72 hour burn-in testing at elevated temperatures
  • Performance test reports - Third-party or in-house verification of read/write speeds and IOPS
  • Compliance with WEEE directive - Required for EU markets, covering end-of-life product recycling
