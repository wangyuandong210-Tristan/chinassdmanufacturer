SSD OEM MOQ Guide: Minimum Order Quantities for Private Label SSDs

July 16, 2026 · 10 min read

If you are starting your own SSD brand, the first question is usually: "What is the minimum order quantity (MOQ) for OEM SSDs?" The answer depends on the manufacturer, the product type, and the level of customization you need. This guide breaks down typical MOQs and how to negotiate the best terms.

What Affects OEM MOQ?

Several factors determine a factory's MOQ requirements:

  • Custom labeling - If you only need standard products with your brand label, MOQ is lower (100-200 units).
  • Custom packaging - Retail box design increases MOQ since factories need to order printed boxes in bulk.
  • Firmware customization - This requires engineering time, so factories typically ask for higher MOQ (500+ units).
  • Hardware customization - Custom PCB layout or component selection is the highest MOQ tier (1000+ units).

Common OEM MOQ Scenarios

Here are real scenarios based on common buyer situations:

Scenario 1: New brand, testing the market
Start with 100-200 units of 2-3 SKUs. Focus on the most popular capacities (512GB and 1TB NVMe). Use standard product with custom label only. Expected MOQ: 200 units per SKU.

Scenario 2: Established brand, expanding lineup
Order 500-1000 units per SKU with custom packaging. Negotiate for firmware customization and longer warranty. Expected MOQ: 500 units per SKU.

Scenario 3: Distributor, large volume
Order 2000-5000 units per SKU across multiple product lines. This qualifies for the best pricing and full customization. Expected MOQ: 1000-2000 units per SKU.
