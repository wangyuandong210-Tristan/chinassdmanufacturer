SSD OEM/ODM Guide: How to Start Your Own SSD Brand

July 1, 2025 · 14 min read

Starting your own SSD brand is more accessible than ever. With China's mature manufacturing ecosystem, you can launch a professional SSD line with your own branding, packaging, and specifications without owning a factory. This guide walks through every step of the SSD OEM/ODM process.

What is SSD OEM/ODM?

OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturing) and ODM (Original Design Manufacturing) are two partnership models with factories:

FeatureOEM (White Label)ODM (Custom Design)
Product designChoose from existing modelsCustom design based on your specs
BrandingCustom label + packagingFull custom branding + firmware
MOQ100-200 units per SKU500-1000 units per SKU
Lead time15-20 days25-40 days
Cost per unitLower (standard design)Higher (custom NRE fees)
Best forNew brands, quick launchEstablished brands, differentiation

Step 1: Choose Your OEM/ODM Partner

The right factory partner is the most important decision. Look for these criteria:

  • Factory ownership - Verify they own their production facility, not just a trading company. Ask for a video call or factory visit.
  • NAND sourcing - Reputable manufacturers use branded NAND from Samsung, Micron, Kioxia, or YMTC. Ask for supply chain documentation.
  • Quality control - Look for factories with burn-in testing, 100% product testing, and quality management systems.
  • Certification capability - Your OEM partner should handle CE, FCC, ROHS certification for your brand.
  • Communication - English-speaking sales team, responsive communication, and clear project management.

Step 2: Define Your Product Specifications

Work with your OEM partner to define the following:

SpecificationOptionsConsiderations
InterfaceNVMe PCIe 3.0/4.0, SATA IIINVMe for speed, SATA for compatibility
NAND typeTLC, QLC, MLCTLC for mainstream, QLC for budget
Capacity256GB, 512GB, 1TB, 2TB, 4TBHigher capacities = higher margins
Warranty1-5 years3 years standard for OEM

Step 3: Branding and Packaging

Your OEM partner can handle complete branding:

  • Drive labeling - Custom labels with your logo, capacity, and model number
  • Firmware customization - Your brand name appears as the drive model in Windows/macOS
  • Retail packaging - Custom box design, blister packs, or bulk packaging
  • Certification - CE/FCC/ROHS certificates issued under your brand name

Step 4: Quality Control and Testing

Quality is critical for your brand reputation. Ensure your partner provides:

  • 100% burn-in testing at high temperature before shipment
  • Performance verification (CrystalDiskMark or equivalent)
  • Compatibility testing with common platforms (Intel, AMD, various chipsets)
  • Final visual inspection and packaging quality check
  • Sample approval process before mass production

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between OEM and ODM for SSDs?

OEM means you choose from existing product designs with your branding. ODM means the factory develops a custom design based on your specific requirements. OEM is faster and cheaper; ODM offers more differentiation.

Do I need to visit the factory in China?

While not strictly required, a factory visit is highly recommended for building trust and understanding production capabilities. Many partners can also provide video tours.

Can I start with small quantities?

Most OEM partners offer trial orders with 50-100 units for new partners. This allows you to validate the product and packaging before committing to larger volumes.

How much does it cost to start an SSD brand?

Starting costs are relatively low for OEM. The main costs are product MOQ, label/package design, certification filing, and samples. A trial run of 500 units might cost $5,000-$15,000 depending on specifications.
