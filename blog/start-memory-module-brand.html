How to Start Your Own Memory Module Brand: DDR4/DDR5 OEM Guide

July 17, 2026 · 10 min read

The global memory module market is projected to reach $200B+ by 2030. Starting your own DDR4 or DDR5 brand through OEM/ODM manufacturing is one of the most accessible entry points into the storage industry. This guide covers everything from partner selection to market launch.

Market Opportunity for Memory OEM Brands

The demand for branded memory modules continues to grow across segments:

  • PC upgrade market: DDR4 remains strong for budget builds while DDR5 dominates premium
  • Industrial/embedded: Long-lifecycle demand for specific DDR4 specifications
  • Server/data center: DDR5 ECC and RDIMM modules for growing cloud infrastructure
  • Regional brands: Local brands in emerging markets are growing faster than global giants

Step-by-Step OEM Process

Step 1: Define your product line
Start with the most popular SKUs: 8GB and 16GB DDR4/DDR5 modules at standard speeds. These have the widest market and lowest manufacturing risk.

Step 2: Select your OEM partner
Look for factories with: in-house IC testing, 100% module testing, ability to provide CE/FCC/ROHS under your brand, and willingness to accept small initial orders.

Step 3: Customize your branding
Design your brand label, retail packaging, and optional heat spreader. Most OEM partners have design templates to speed up this process.

Step 4: Certification and testing
Your OEM partner should provide certifications with your brand name. Request test reports for compatibility with major motherboard platforms.

Step 5: Launch and scale
Start with 2-3 SKUs. Based on sell-through data, expand to higher capacities and speeds. Most successful memory brands start small and grow product lines over time.
